Until I was 26 years old, I lived my life believing I was stupid.

Believing that I wasn’t good enough.

Believing that there was something wrong with me.

...

I did everything I could to try to prove to myself that those beliefs weren’t true.

And when I got too tired of trying, I’d just run from the pain of those beliefs using any distraction - regardless of the consequences.

“It ain't what you don't know that gets you into trouble. It's what you know for sure that just ain't so.” - Mark Twain

———

Even with years of internal and external work on myself, I still struggle at times with that voice inside my head.

I doubt it will ever go away completely.

At first this realization made me angry, but now I’m grateful for the reminders.

You know that old saying about not feeling the sunshine without the rain? Yeah, that’s the one.

———-

I’d like your help.

This video is used to help me quickly provide event organizers with an idea of who I am, and my speaker style. Based on if they feel it’s a good fit for their organization, they book me to deliver an event.

Through these speeches, workshops and retreats I help families to prevent or correct the patterns that create the types unhealthy beliefs I held onto for years. Beliefs that lead me to a place where I was facing three years in prison and suicidal.

———-

I’d love your feedback...as I’m always striving to do, and be better.

I’m beyond grateful that I am mostly booked out for 2019, but I’d love to use this video (once its complete) to drive more bookings, and be able to positively impact even more families.

Please, please, please understand that I crave honest and direct feedback. Both strengths and weaknesses.

You will not hurt my feelings by telling me you don’t like the video as a whole, or certain parts of it. Maybe you don’t like the music, maybe you hate the shot of me smiling at what feels like the wrong moment give what I’m saying.

My point is, what hurts my feelings is when people don’t give me honest feedback so I can continue to raise the bar and improve.

So please, if you feel called to, watch this video and provide your feedback so I can ultimately help more families.

Note, we will be adding captions once we have a final edit.

Love,

Bradley

Ps. For those of you in EO or YPO, specific feedback from pass learning chairs/ officers is appreciated
