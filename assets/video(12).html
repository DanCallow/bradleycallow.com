To all my mental and behavioral health colleagues...I am sad.

I am afraid for our field...and in this case, it isn't regarding the overwhelming amount of fraud seen via insurance driven programs.

It is in regards to the limited number of our colleagues willing to think outside their traditional treatment box.

...

People are dying every single day, and I ask myself, are we doing everything possible to reduce the pain experienced by families, friends, and the individuals themselves?

Addiction, as we all know, is cunning and baffling. The idea that just because you got sober or got control of your substance misuse in a certain way automatically translates to the idea that everyone else's journey will mirror yours is inconceivable to me.

The reality is that 90% of people struggling with substance use disorders aren't seeking ANY kind of help.

This lends itself to the idea that the options we have available are not welcoming or attractive enough for the majority of those suffering.

We can continue to say, "they're just not ready" or we can extend our hand to allow them to progressively realize the gifts of changing their behavior.

If everyone in this industry really wants to help people...why don't programs possess a research and development department designed to help us find a cure?

Get out of your box. What got us here will alone NOT get us to a place where 90% of people suffering is a statistic we could never believe was a reality.

For more abou what we are doing...check out www.TheModerationInstitute.com and if you feel called, please like our Facebook page.
